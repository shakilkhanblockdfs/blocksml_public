# AI Fluency Evaluation Key benefits How AI Fluency works Enabling AI Fluency evaluation Viewing AI Fluency results Frequently Asked Question (FAQ)

_Last updated: Feb 11, 2026, 4:07 PM (Last updated 2 months ago)_

This feature is part of the AI Add-on. For more information, see [📄 Advanced Evaluation](/articles/7098008997).

AI Fluency measures how effectively candidates use AI assistants during assessments. 

# Key benefits

AI Fluency Evaluation provides the following benefits:

- **AI collaboration competency:** Distinguishes between strategic AI use and overreliance by assessing how candidates integrate AI tools into their problem-solving approach.

- **Quality over frequency:** Measures how effectively candidates communicate requirements and apply AI suggestions, rather than tracking usage frequency.

- **Enhanced differentiation:** Reveals differences in problem-solving approaches and tool-usage strategies among candidates with similar coding scores.

- **Modern engineering readiness:** Indicates a candidate’s ability to iterate on solutions and build on previous work

**Note:** AI Fluency Evaluation supports the following question types:

- Coding

- Projects

# How AI Fluency works

AI Fluency uses large language models (LLMs) and standard rubrics to evaluate behavioral patterns in candidate-AI interactions.

The system analyzes the full conversation history between the candidate and the AI assistant, including prompts, actions, and responses. 

The AI Fluency evaluation focuses on the following dimensions:

- **Context quality:** How clearly and effectively candidates communicate requirements and provide relevant technical context.

- **Critical thinking:** Evidence of independent reasoning and analysis, with AI used as a support tool rather than a replacement for judgment.

- **Collaboration:** How candidates progress through problems, build on earlier interactions, and refine solutions over time.

## AI Fluency grades

AI Fluency assigns one of the following grades based on how candidates interact with the AI assistant:

- **Grade A (High AI Fluency):** Demonstrates effective AI collaboration through strategic tool use, clear communication, independent analysis, and iterative refinement.

- **Grade B (Moderate AI Fluency):** Shows reasonable AI collaboration with developing proficiency. Indicates opportunities to improve consistency or depth.

- **Grade C (Low AI Fluency):** Indicates limited AI collaboration and a need to improve effective use of AI tools.

**Note:** The evaluation applies the same standards to all candidates, regardless of experience level.

# Enabling AI Fluency evaluation

To enable AI Fluency:

1.  Enable **Advanced Evaluation** in the test-level settings. For more information, see [Enable Advanced Evaluation](https://support.hackerrank.com/articles/7098008997#enable-advanced-evaluation).

    **Note:** AI Fluency Evaluation is part of the **Advanced Evaluation** package and cannot be enabled independently.

<!-- -->

1.  Enable **Proctor Mode** in the test-level settings. For more information, see [Enable Proctor Mode](https://support.hackerrank.com/articles/5663779659-proctor-mode#enable-proctor-mode).

# Viewing AI Fluency results

AI Fluency results appear in both the **Summary Report** and the **Detailed Report**.

2.  Scroll to the relevant **Coding** or **Projects** question.

3.  Click the question to open the **Detailed Report**.

# Frequently Asked Question (FAQ)

**Is AI Fluency available for every test?**

No. AI Fluency is available only when **Advanced Evaluation** and **Proctor Mode** are enabled for the test.
